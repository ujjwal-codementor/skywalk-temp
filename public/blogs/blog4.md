## Why Furniture Insurance Falls Short

Let’s be honest: insurance sounds reassuring — until you read the fine print. Here’s what typically happens with furniture insurance:

### • **Limited Coverage**  
Most policies only cover accidental or catastrophic events. Everyday wear, fading, or scuffs are excluded.

### • **Deductibles and Appraisals**  
You pay out of pocket first, then wait for approval and reimbursement.

### • **Claim Hassles**  
Expect forms, photos, inspections, and weeks of waiting.

### • **Depreciation Rules**  
Older furniture is valued less, so even approved claims may not cover replacement or repair costs.

For luxury homeowners, these limitations are more than inconveniences — they’re deal-breakers. After all, what good is “coverage” if it doesn’t preserve what you love most?
