## The Limitations of Traditional Furniture Insurance

Even the most comprehensive insurance plan has gaps. Here’s why many high-end homeowners find insurance lacking:

### 1. High Costs and Deductibles
Insurance premiums for furniture can be expensive, and claims often come with deductibles. For luxury furniture, small damages might cost more than the deductible, making insurance economically inefficient.

### 2. Reactive Rather Than Preventive
Most policies only respond after damage occurs — meaning scratches, stains, and minor wear go unaddressed until it’s too late. By then, your furniture may require expensive restoration.

### 3. Complex Claims Processes
Filing claims often involves lengthy paperwork, photos, damage assessments, and waiting periods. For busy homeowners, this is stressful and time-consuming — especially when the damage could have been prevented.

### 4. Limited Coverage
Insurance may exclude certain types of wear or cosmetic damage, leaving gaps in protection. It rarely offers ongoing maintenance, the kind of care that preserves both aesthetics and value.
